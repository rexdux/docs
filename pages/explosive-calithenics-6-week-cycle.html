Explosive Calisthenics — 6-Week Cycle

High-intensity, low-volume bodyweight exercises structured for repeatability

COLE, Thomas. Home in the Woods. 1847.

Introduction

This is a high-intensity calisthenics cycle lasting 6 consecutive weeks, built on low structural volume and controlled metabolic density.

In the fifth week, push yourself to the limit to reach technical failure1 and try to beat your personal rep records.

To avoid central nervous system burnout and injuries, a complete one-week deep rest period is mandatory in the sixth week. After that, you can restart the cycle whenever you want.

Pre-Session

Perform 30 s of prehab exercises to increase blood flow, joint centration, and neural readiness of each of the main joints involved in the daily session:

  • Pull/Push days: Wrists, shoulders, scapula, and neck.
  • Leg day: Ankles, knees, and hips.
  • Every session: Band External Rotation, Band Face Pull, and Band Pull-Apart.

Daily Sessions

The order of the daily sessions is Pull—Leg—Push. Rest on the fourth day to reduce accumulated fatigue, and then repeat the process from the first day.

Perform a total of 3 progressive sets2 to awaken your max neuromuscular drive for the target exercise of the daily session:

  • Activation: Perform 1 set of 15 s of isometric hold with moderate contraction. Rest 30 s.
  • Potentiation I: Perform 1 set of half3 of the working set reps (max 6 reps) at a controlled pace. Rest 1 min.
  • Potentiation II: Perform 1 set of one-quarter of the working set reps (max 3 reps) at an explosive pace. Rest 3 min.

Perform a total of 4 full sets to drive long-term max strength gains for the target exercise of the daily session:

  • Working Sets: Perform 4 sets at an explosive pace with progressive overload4. Rest 5 min.

Post-Session

Perform a total of 10 static fascia stretches to reduce passive tissue restrictions and preserve long-term training capacity across cycles per target muscle:

  • Moderate Fascia Stretching: Perform 3 sets of light to moderate static stretching7, gradually increasing duration and intensity. Rest 15 s.
  • Deep Fascia Stretching: Perform 7 sets of 30 s of linear, deep static fascia stretching. Rest 15 s.

Perform a total of 3 static forearm stretches to accelerate recovery at the end of each daily session:

  • Forearm Stretching: Perform 3 sets of light to moderate static stretching. Rest 15 s.

  1. Technical failure: This occurs when your rep speed noticeably decreases or your form worsens. Stop immediately; do not grind reps.↩︎

  2. Progressive sets: If you feel any sharp pain, stop immediately and postpone the session until the next day.↩︎

  3. Fraction of reps: If the calculation results in a decimal number, round down.↩︎

  4. Progressive overload: In the last set, if you feel you could have done two or more reps before technical failure, add 1 rep to the exercise in the next session.↩︎

  5. Band-assisted: If you don’t have a resistance band, use your legs for support on the floor or a bench to reduce the load.↩︎

  6. Bodyweight: If you can’t do the bodyweight version yet, start with band-assisted and then progress to negative pull-ups and dips.↩︎

  7. Static stretching: Never do intense or deep stretches in the muscles you will be training the next day.↩︎
