2-Set Method

Strength first. Skills after.

COLE, Thomas. The Return. 1837.

Stage I

Reach to 15 bodyweight pull-ups and 20 bodyweight dips. Explosive concentric and controlled eccentric. Rest 5–7 min after each set.

If you can’t do bodyweight pull-ups or dips, start with negative, band-assisted or machine-assisted pull-ups and dips.

Stage II

Warm-Up

Before starting each compound exercise, perform 3–5 min of dynamic stretching and 2 warm-up sets. Rest 3–4 min after each set.

Level Set I Set II
Beginner 5 band-assisted or machine-assisted reps 5 bodyweight reps
Default 5 reps at a load strictly below 50% of the working-set weight 5 reps at a load strictly below 75% of the working-set weight

Warm-up rule: For each warm-up set, calculate the target percentage of your working-set load and select the closest available load strictly below that value, using the standard 2.5 kg increment scale.

Working Sets

In compound exercises, perform 2 sets of 5–7 reps to failure. Rest 5–7 min after each set.

In isolation exercises, perform 3 sets of 10–12 reps with 2–3 RIR. Rest 3–4 min after each set.

For all exercises, perform with explosive concentric, controlled eccentric and progressive overload1.

If you’re a beginner, start with weighted dips at 10 kg and weighted pull-ups at 5 kg.

At the end of the 5-day program, repeat from day 1.
